Inverse Simulation of Newtonâ€™s Law of Cooling#

We will use the Newtonâ€™s Law of Cooling to simulate the temperature of a body that is cooling down. The Newtonâ€™s Law of Cooling is given by the following differential equation:

(1)#\[\begin{equation} \frac{dT}{dt} = -k(T - T_a) \end{equation}\]

where:

  • \(T\) is the temperature of the body at time \(t\),

  • \(T_a\) is the ambient temperature,

  • \(k\) is the cooling constant,

  • \(\frac{dT}{dt}\) is the rate of change of temperature with respect to time.

The solution to this differential equation is given by:

(2)#\[\begin{equation} T(t) = T_a + (T_0 - T_a)e^{-kt} \end{equation}\]

We will try to estimate the cooling constant \(k\) in this notebook.
